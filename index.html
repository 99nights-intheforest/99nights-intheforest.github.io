About 99 Nights in the Forest

99 Nights in the Forest is an intense survival horror game where players must endure 99 grueling nights in a mysterious, danger-filled wilderness. Drawing from classic Roblox survival experiences, this 101% original version amps up the terror with cannibalistic deer, shape-shifting Skinwalkers, and relentless bandit raids, challenging you to gather resources, build defenses, and uncover hidden secrets to stay alive.

Features of 99 Nights in the Forest

Resource Gathering and Crafting: Chop trees for wood, hunt animals for pelts and food, and craft tools, weapons, and fortifications. Upgrade your crafting bench early to unlock advanced items like the Infernal Crossbow for battling powerful foes.

Dynamic Night-Day Cycle: Days are for exploration and building, while nights bring horror—keep your campfire fueled to ward off entities. Failing to do so invites attacks from the Cannibal Deer or the Owl, which can stun or chase you relentlessly.

Base Building and Defense: Construct shelters, plant saplings in strategic circles for natural barriers against Cultist invasions, and fortify with traps. Replanting trees prevents resource depletion and creates makeshift defenses.

Exploration and Quests: Venture into dungeons, abandoned buildings, and foggy areas to find loot, maps, and lost children. Rescuing kids reduces the required nights, and trading with NPCs like the Pelt Trader (using items like Rabbit's Feet) yields upgrades such as better axes or sacks.

Class System and Customization: Start with affordable classes like Scavenger for extra inventory space (only 25 Diamonds). Redeem codes for free Diamonds to unlock premium classes, talent rerolls, and cosmetic enhancements.

Horror Elements and Updates: Face jumpscares from flying Owls or demonic Deer; recent patches (e.g., September 22, 2025) introduce volcanic biomes, new entities, and multiplayer co-op for shared base-building.

How to Survive 99 Nights in the Forest

Surviving the full 99 nights requires strategy, caution, and quick thinking. Here's a step-by-step guide blending core mechanics from the wilderness horrors:

  • Start Strong: Upon spawning, immediately chop nearby trees for wood and fuel your campfire to prevent early night terrors. Scout for buildings to loot chairs, coal, or chests for quick resources—open everything before meeting traders for better deals.
  • Prepare for Traders and Upgrades: Hunt rabbits for feet to trade with the Pelt Trader on day two. Prioritize the Good Axe for faster chopping and as a weapon. Avoid wasting Diamonds on flashlights; they're common finds.
  • Build Essentials: Craft a Map (just 3 wood) for easy navigation (press M), an Old Bed to accelerate day progression (+1 multiplier), and upgrade your Crafting Bench to tier 2. Skip bunny traps initially—focus on sustainable tools.
  • Defend Against Threats: Replant saplings around your campfire for a free barrier against bandits and Cultists. At night, shine flashlights to stun The Owl (can't be killed) and run from the Cannibal Deer—it's faster than you, so hide in structures.
  • Explore Wisely: Delve into underground dungeons for valuable loot but watch for Skinwalkers. Hunt wolves and rabbits for sustenance, and rescue lost children to cut down nights. Play co-op to share tasks like base defense.
  • Advanced Tips: Use codes for Diamonds to buy classes or rerolls. In late game, tackle volcanic areas with new weapons against Cultist Kings. Always plan escapes—death resets your base but keeps money for restarts.

What's the best starting class?

Scavenger is ideal for beginners—cheap (25 Diamonds) and offers extra bag space. Upgrade it before buying fancier ones.

Can I kill The Owl or Cannibal Deer?

No, The Owl can only be stunned with a flashlight. The Deer requires evasion; focus on hiding or running to safety.

What happens when I die?

Permadeath wipes your base and resources, but you keep money and survived days for easier restarts.
